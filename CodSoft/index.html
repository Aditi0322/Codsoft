Kalpana Chawla

"First Indian woman in space and a symbol of courage"

About Kalpana Chawla

Kalpana Chawla was an Indian-American astronaut and the first woman of Indian origin to go to space. She was born on March 17, 1962, in Karnal, India. With her determination and brilliance, she became a role model for millions, especially women in science and space exploration.

Admirable Qualities

  • Bravery: She embarked on space missions despite the risks involved.
  • Dedication: Her passion for aerospace led her to reach NASA.
  • Inspiration: She encouraged girls across the world to follow their dreams.
  • Resilience: Faced challenges as a woman in STEM and conquered them.
  • Legacy: Her life and work are celebrated even after her tragic loss in 2003.

Kalpana once said, "The path from dreams to success does exist. May you have the vision to find it." Her story reminds us that with courage and commitment, anything is possible.
